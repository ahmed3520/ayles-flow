# Ayles SEO Page Briefs

This is the content quality bar for Ayles SEO pages.

We are not publishing thin keyword pages.
We are publishing product-led landing pages that a real buyer or creative lead would actually use.

## Non-Negotiables

- Every page must target one real job to be done.
- Every page must include real product proof: screenshots, workflow diagrams, example outputs, or example prompts.
- Every page must explain who the page is for, when Ayles is the right fit, and when it is not.
- Every page must have a clear CTA tied to the product, not a vague "learn more".
- Every comparison page must be honest and specific.
- If a page would feel useless without Google, do not publish it.

## Shared Page Structure

Use this structure for all major landing pages:

1. Hero
   - Clear category statement
   - One-sentence value prop
   - CTA
   - Screenshot or product visual
2. Why this matters
   - Explain the workflow problem in plain language
3. How Ayles works
   - Show the connected flow, not just isolated features
4. Real workflow example
   - Inputs
   - Steps
   - Outputs
   - Models used
5. Why Ayles is different
   - Visual canvas
   - Connected multimodal pipeline
   - Agent assistance
   - Research / PDF / text / media handoff where relevant
6. Use cases
   - 3-5 concrete jobs
7. FAQ
8. Final CTA

## Writing Rules

- Avoid empty claims like "revolutionary", "game-changing", or "best-in-class" unless backed by specifics.
- Use concrete nouns and verbs.
- Prefer "connect image to video" over "unlock powerful multimedia experiences".
- Show the workflow early.
- Keep intros tight.
- Do not stuff every related keyword into the first screen.

## What Not to Publish

- Thin city-swap or audience-swap pages
- Generic "top tools" listicles with no product truth
- Auto-generated comparison pages with no screenshots or proof
- Empty landing pages built only to capture a phrase
- Pages that repeat the same body copy with tiny keyword swaps
